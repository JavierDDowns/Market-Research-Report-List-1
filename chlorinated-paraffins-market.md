<p><p>Chlorinated paraffins are categorized by their chlorine content and chain length, resulting in types like CP-42, CP-52, and CP-70. CP-42 has lower chlorine content, making it suitable for applications requiring flexibility and lower viscosity, such as in plastics. CP-52 offers a moderate balance, often used in rubber and adhesives, while CP-70 with higher chlorine content provides enhanced flame resistance, commonly found in industrial lubricants and coatings. Other types may include varied formulations for niche applications.</p></p>
