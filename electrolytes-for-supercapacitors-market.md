<p><p>The electrolytes for supercapacitors can be categorized into three main types: aqueous, organic, and ionic liquids. Aqueous electrolytes use water as a solvent and provide high conductivity but have lower voltage limits. Organic electrolytes utilize organic solvents, offering higher voltage and energy density but with potential safety concerns. Ionic liquids are composed of salts in a liquid state at room temperature, boasting excellent thermal stability and wide electrochemical windows, making them suitable for high-performance applications.</p></p>
